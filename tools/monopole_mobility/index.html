How to Measure

  1. Seal the soundhole – Use tape or a food container lid to make it airtight.
  2. Mute the strings, tap the top, and record the uncoupled top frequency (fu) with your FFT app.
  3. Mount the mobility jig – Hang a known test mass (m) vertically over the bridge and measure deflection (x) in millimeters.
  4. The deflection should be small but elastic — don’t crush the top.
  5. Enter your readings above – The calculator converts x → meters, computes stiffness (K), effective modal mass (Meff), and mobility.
  6. The score tells you how freely the top moves air.
Field Notes
  • Make sure the soundhole seal is airtight before tapping.
  • Input deflection in millimeters — most dial gauges read tiny movements.
  • Keep mass in kilograms (no grams conversion here).
  • Apply load vertically at the bridge; avoid rocking or twisting.
